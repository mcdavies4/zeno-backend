Security at Zeno

We take the security of your money and personal data seriously. Here's exactly how we protect you.

How we protect your money

🔐

PIN-Protected Transactions

Every payment requires your 4-digit PIN. Your PIN is hashed using bcrypt — even we cannot see it. After 3 incorrect attempts, your account is automatically locked.

🛡️

256-bit TLS Encryption

All data transmitted between your WhatsApp, our servers, and your bank is encrypted using industry-standard TLS 1.3. Your data is unreadable in transit.

🏦

Open Banking — Read Only

We connect to your bank via FCA-regulated Open Banking. This gives us read-only access to your balance and transactions. We cannot move money without your explicit instruction and PIN.

🪪

Identity Verification

Every Zeno user completes KYC verification via Veriff. We verify your government-issued ID and perform a liveness check to prevent identity fraud.

❄️

Instant Account Freeze

If your phone is lost or stolen, you can freeze your Zeno account instantly by messaging us from any device. All payment activity stops immediately.

🔍

Transaction Monitoring

We monitor transactions in real time for unusual patterns. Suspicious activity triggers automatic alerts and may result in temporary holds for your protection.

What happens when you make a payment

1

You request a payment

You send a natural language message to Zeno, e.g. "Send £50 to John Smith".

2

Zeno confirms the details

We show you a summary of the transfer — recipient, amount, bank, and reference — before anything happens.

3

You enter your PIN

You must enter your 4-digit PIN to authorise the payment. No PIN, no payment.

4

Payment is processed

Only after PIN confirmation do we submit the payment via Faster Payments. You receive a confirmation with a transaction ID.

5

You get a receipt

We send you a full transaction receipt including the new balance and a unique transaction reference.

Tips to keep your account safe

✓

Never share your PIN

Zeno staff will never ask for your PIN. Anyone asking for it is attempting fraud.

✓

Lock your WhatsApp

Enable WhatsApp's built-in fingerprint or face ID lock in Settings → Privacy → Screen Lock.

✓

Check payment details carefully

Always verify the recipient name, sort code and account number before confirming a payment.

✓

Report suspicious activity

If you notice transactions you don't recognise, contact us immediately at [email protected].

Found a security issue?

We take security reports seriously. If you've discovered a vulnerability in Zeno, please contact our security team responsibly and we'll investigate promptly.
